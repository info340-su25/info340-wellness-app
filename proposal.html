Project Proposal: HuskyHealth Tracker

A simple, student-focused wellness tracker for sleep, diet, exercise, and mood.

Problem & Data Domain

College students, especially those navigating busy schedules at a large university like UW, often struggle to maintain consistent wellness habits. Sleep deprivation, poor hydration, elevated stress, and irregular fitness routines are common but under-tracked behaviors.

While commercial wellness apps like MyFitnessPal or Daylio exist, they are often too complex, bloated with features, or require subscriptions. Most importantly, they do not center the student experience or the daily challenges that come with academic life.

There is a clear information gap: UW students need a simple, centralized tool to self-track and reflect on their well-being. Our web app will fill this gap by providing a way to monitor wellness metrics relevant to student life. This app aims to promote healthier habits, mindfulness, and data-driven reflection.

Proposed Application

Our application is a wellness dashboard built for UW students. It allows users to log their sleep, diet, exercise, and mood.

The primary users are college students aged 18 to 25. The interface will be inclusive, simple, and private. Accessibility features like semantic HTML, proper color contrast, and alt-text will ensure a better experience.

Privacy will be emphasized, with all data stored securely and no third-party integrations required.

Key Interactive Features

  • Daily Log Pages – Users can log sleep, diet, exercise, and mood via simple forms.
  • Streak & Habit Tracker – Users can view trends, track habit streaks, and see their progress visually over time.
  • Diary Section – Users can reflect on their day with free-form journal entries linked to their logged data.

All data will be user-generated and stored persistently. No external APIs or integrations are required.
