Project Proposal: UW Wellness Tracker

Problem / Data Domain

College students, especially those navigating busy schedules at a large university like UW, often struggle to maintain consistent wellness habits. Sleep deprivation, poor hydration, elevated stress, and irregular fitness routines are common but under-tracked behaviors. While commercial wellness apps like MyFitnessPal or Daylio exist, they are often too complex, bloated with features, or require integrations with wearable devices or subscriptions. Most importantly, they do not center the student experience or the daily challenges that come with academic life.

There is a clear information gap: UW students need a simple, centralized tool to self-track and reflect on their well-being. Our web app will fill this gap by providing a low-friction, privacy-centered way to monitor wellness metrics relevant to student life. This app aims to promote healthier habits, mindfulness, and data-driven reflection, without overwhelming the user.

Proposed Application

Our application will be a wellness dashboard built for UW students. It will allow users to log their sleep, water intake, mood, stress, and exercise behaviors.

The primary users are college students aged 18â€“25. The interface will be inclusive, simple, and private. Inclusivity in our app would mean having accessibility features such as semantic HTML, proper color contrast, and alt-text. Users will have privacy through being able to have a login to safeguard their personal information.

Our interactive features will include:

  • Form for entering and tracking goals, and shows progress throughout the set time duration. Units are able to be selected, such as kilograms vs. pounds, or distance traveled (miles vs. kilometer).
  • A graph/calender view that shows user's wellness trends over time
  • A "diary" portion where users can log their thoughts/feelings on their performance day-to-day. If progress doesn't meet set goals at the end of the day, extra prompts will be added towards the diary portion. After logging information into the form at the end of the day, the user will be prompted by the Wellness Tracker to fill out the diary portion.

All data will be user-generated and stored persistently. No external APIs or integrations are required, keeping the system secure and in scope for the class. This project will demonstrate how simple information design can promote personal transformation through consistent interaction.
